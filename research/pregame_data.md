## 3. Historical Upset Data by Seed Matchup

> Source: Wikipedia — "NCAA Division I men's basketball tournament upsets" article. Data current through 2025 tournament (164 games per matchup since 1985 expansion to 64 teams). **Updated through 2025 (pre-2026 tournament).**

| Matchup | Higher Seed Win % | Lower Seed Win % | Notable |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|---------|
| 1 vs 16 | **98.8%** | **1.2%** (2 wins) | UMBC 2018, FDU 2023 |
| 2 vs 15 | **93.3%** | **6.7%** (11 wins) | Most recent: Princeton 2023 |
| 3 vs 14 | **85.4%** | **14.6%** (21+ wins) | Happens roughly once per year |
| 4 vs 13 | **79.3%** | **20.7%** (27+ wins) | ~1 per year on average |
| 5 vs 12 | **64.6%** | **35.4%** (36+ wins) | Classic "upset special" — happens ~1.4x/year |
| 6 vs 11 | **62.2%** | **37.8%** (36+ wins) | First Four 11-seeds often dangerous |
| 7 vs 10 | **60.4%** | **39.6%** | Near coin-flip territory |
| 8 vs 9 | **51.8%** | **48.2%** | Essentially a coin flip |

### Key Historical Patterns:
- **5 vs 12 is the classic upset matchup**: ~35% upset rate, most reliable "bracket buster"
- **8 vs 9 is a coin flip**: Don't assign much weight to seeding here
- **1 seeds are nearly untouchable**: Only 2 losses to 16 seeds in 164 games
- **2 vs 15 has become more common**: 11 upsets, with 3 in the 2020s alone (Oral Roberts 2021, Saint Peter's 2022, Princeton 2023)
- **11 seeds from First Four**: VCU (2011), UCLA (2021), NC State (2024) made deep runs as 11 seeds
- **Double-digit seeds in Sweet 16**: Has happened 18 consecutive years (2008-2025), at least one each year

### Key Historical Facts for Model Training

**Since 1985 (64-team era):**
- 1 seeds: ~99% first-round win rate, ~85% reach Sweet 16, ~50% reach Final Four
- 2 seeds: ~94% first-round win rate, ~66% reach Sweet 16
- Only 2 times has a 16 beaten a 1 (1.2%)
- 12 over 5 upsets happen ~35% of the time
- At least one double-digit seed has made Sweet 16 for 18 straight years
- Conference tournament champions from mid-majors who enter with momentum are upset risks as 12-13 seeds
- Teams with strong SOS and moderate records (e.g., 4-seeds from power conferences) are more vulnerable than their seed suggests
